Abstract

In diffusion-based text-to-music (TTM) generation, one typical approach involves a cascaded system that starts with a text-to-spectrogram diffusion-based generator, followed by a phase reconstructor (vocoder) to convert the spectrogram into a waveform. Commonly used vocoders produce monophonic audio at lower resolutions (e.g. 16-24kHz), which limits their use for applications requiring high-fidelity sound. To address this issue, we propose MusicHiFi - a new vocoder for high-fidelity stereophonic sound generation. Our method employs a cascade of three generative adversarial network-based models (GAN-triplet), each designed to progressively enhance the fidelity of the generated music. Initially, we convert spectrograms to low-resolution audio. This low-resolution audio is then upsampled to high-resolution monophonic audio using a bandwidth expander. The final step involves converting this mono high-resolution audio into stereophonic high-resolution audio. Compared to previous work, our method integrates a unified GAN-based discriminator and generator design, complemented by a novel cycle-consistent mono-to-stereo module. By comparing both objective and subjective fidelity metrics on out-of-domain music datasets, our approach not only achieves competitive fidelity with remarkable efficiency, but also produces interpretable and listenable intermediate outputs.

Examples

We provide two groups of the samples demonstrating the high-fidelity stereo vocoding for music generation. The first group is focused on overall system performance on both generated music spectrogram and genuine music spectrogram. The second group concentrates on the intermediate output from different vocoding stages, including bandwidth extension and controllable mono-to-stereo upmixing. All of the genuine music spectrograms are from the licensed music, and the audio samples are in the format of mp3.
